Bohemian Pilsner

Description

The traditional Bohemian Pilsner is crisp, dry, bright, and effervescent with a dense white foam head and hints of diacetyl

Pilsner has shorter history than some beers but has been around for over 150 years now, it remains one of the most popular styles of beer for a reason

Ingredients

Malt

  • 8 lbs Floor Malted Pilsner
  • 4 lbs Chit Malt
  • 8 oz Raw Wheat
  • 4 oz Acidulated Malt

    • Boil Hops

  • 90 g Czech Saaz
  • 35 g Hallertau Tradition

    • Dry Hops

  • 30 g Czech Saaz

    • Yeast

  • 11.5 g S-189 Lager Dry Yeast

    • Directions

    1. Mash for 60 minutes at 150*F
    2. Sparge, ensuring run-off takes approximately 90 minutes netting a kettle volume of 7 gallons
    3. Boil vigoriously for 90 minutes, ensuring the boil hops are added at the correct times
    4. Cool wort, knock out at 48*F to allow the yeast to begin fermentation by morning
    5. After 14 days add the dry hop additions
    6. Once fermentation is complete crash the beer down to 32*F
    7. After 2 weeks transfer beer to brite tank for further conditioning
    8. Krausen brite tank with 1/6 of the volume fresh wort, seal the tank during the ensuing fermentation to allow for Co2 production
    9. Once carbonated allow yeast to flocculate for 2 weeks then transfer to another brite tank for packaging
